## What Changed

### 1. Clio rolls out agentic AI to its mass-market installed base

**What happened:** Clio launched agentic AI capabilities across its Work and Vincent platforms, moving from AI-assisted features to autonomous agent-based workflows available to its large small/mid-market law firm customer base.

**What's new:** Until now, agentic AI in legal has been a frontier phenomenon — Harvey's Spectre targeting elite Am Law firms, or experimental deployments at sophisticated corporate legal departments. Clio's release changes the distribution math. This is the first time a platform with broad horizontal penetration across small and mid-sized firms has shipped autonomous agent capabilities as a production feature, not a pilot. The agentic wave is no longer confined to the top of the market.

**Why it matters:** The competitive implications run in two directions. First, for managed review and litigation support providers serving the small/mid-market, this introduces a new category of demand compression risk: if a firm's practice management platform can autonomously handle workflows that previously required LSP engagement, the volume of work flowing to service providers shrinks at the source. This is distinct from the Harvey/frontier vendor threat, which targets elite firm institutional operations. Second, it intensifies the urgency of the governance differentiation thesis. When platforms ship agents natively, the remaining LSP value proposition concentrates further on governed orchestration — the ability to provide auditable, defensible, cross-platform workflow management that a single vendor's embedded agent cannot. LSPs whose service model depends on small/mid-market volume without a governance or orchestration layer face the most immediate exposure. [Sources: 1]

## Watch Next

- **Whether Clio's agent capabilities reduce measurable LSP engagement among small/mid-market firms within the next two quarters** — specifically, whether practice management-embedded agents begin handling document review, intake triage, or compliance workflows that previously required outside service providers. Early usage data or customer case studies from Clio would be the confirming signal.
