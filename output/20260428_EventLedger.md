| **fact_summary** | In *White v. Walmart, Inc.*, Magistrate Judge Tim A. Baker of the Southern District of Indiana ruled that "Artificial intelligence can be a useful discovery tool. The court ordered Plaintiff to supplement discovery responses by April 27, 2026. This is the first federal court ruling explicitly addressing the adequacy standard for AI-assisted discovery — prior rulings addressed hallucination (sanctions), privilege (three-court split), and disclosure requirements, but none had directly ruled on whether AI-generated discovery responses satisfy discovery obligations without independent attorney oversight. The ruling establishes that courts will evaluate AI-assisted discovery responses under a human-oversight adequacy standard, not a technology-capability standard. | Fannie Mae issued Lender Letter LL-2026-04, establishing a formal governance framework for all single-family sellers and servicers using artificial intelligence and machine learning in origination and servicing practices. The framework sets specific governance requirements that the entire Fannie Mae seller/servicer ecosystem must satisfy when deploying AI/ML tools. This represents the first major government-sponsored enterprise (GSE) AI governance mandate, extending AI governance enforcement into regulated financial services through the GSE's contractual authority over its seller/servicer network. Given Fannie Mae's scale (backing approximately 30% of U.S. mortgages), this mandate immediately affects thousands of lenders, servicers, and their legal advisors. |
