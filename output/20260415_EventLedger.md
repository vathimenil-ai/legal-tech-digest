### Compound Signal Note

Today's three Tier 1 developments — K&L Gates ISO 42001 certification, Microsoft Copilot targeting lawyers, and California attorney license suspension for AI misuse — form a compound signal that should be read together:

1. **Governance is hardening** (ISO 42001 certification + license suspension): The floor for acceptable AI governance in legal is rising from self-assessed "best practices" to externally audited standards and enforced with career-ending consequences.

2. **Platform incumbents are moving** (Microsoft Copilot + iManage inference layer): The two most ubiquitously deployed platforms in legal (Microsoft 365 and iManage) are both adding AI capabilities targeted at lawyers, from positions of pre-existing deployment — no new procurement required.

3. **The LSP positioning window is narrowing**: If law firms self-certify governance (K&L Gates model) and platform incumbents provide AI capabilities (Microsoft + iManage model), the LSP value proposition must increasingly rest on complex matter orchestration, cross-platform integration, and domain expertise that neither platform vendors nor law firms can self-supply. The simpler the AI task, the less the LSP is needed.
